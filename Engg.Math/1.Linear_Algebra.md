# Matrix Properties & Eigenvalues

### Key Definitions
* **The Trace:** The sum of the elements on the **main diagonal**. The Trace is always equal to the **Sum of the Eigenvalues**.
* **The Determinant:** A number representing how much a matrix scales an area or volume. The Determinant is always equal to the **Product of the Eigenvalues**.
    * *Example:* If a $3 \times 3$ matrix has eigenvalues of $1$, $2$, and $5$, the Determinant is $1 \times 2 \times 5 = 10$.
* **Singular Matrix:** A matrix with a determinant of exactly $0$. It "collapses" and has **no Inverse**.



# Finding the Determinant of a 3x3 Matrix

The safest way to find a $3 \times 3$ determinant is the **Cofactor Expansion** (or "Cross-Out") method. It breaks one big matrix into three tiny, easy $2 \times 2$ matrices.

### The Prerequisite: The 2×2 Rule
Before solving a $3 \times 3$, you must know how to solve a $2 \times 2$ using cross-multiplication: multiply the main diagonal, and subtract the opposite diagonal.

$$
\text{Det} \begin{vmatrix} 
a & b \\ 
c & d 
\end{vmatrix} = (ad) - (bc)
$$

### The 3×3 "Cross-Out" Method

We focus entirely on the **Top Row** of the matrix. Remember the **Checkerboard Pattern**? The signs for the top row are always **+, -, +**. You must apply these signs to the numbers you pick.



Suppose we have this matrix:

$$
A = \begin{bmatrix} 
a & b & c \\ 
d & e & f \\ 
g & h & i 
\end{bmatrix}
$$

#### Step 1: The Top-Left (+a)
Take the top-left number ($a$). Cross out its entire row and column. Multiply $a$ by the determinant of the remaining $2 \times 2$ matrix.

$$
+a \times \text{Det} \begin{vmatrix} 
e & f \\ 
h & i 
\end{vmatrix}
$$

#### Step 2: The Top-Middle (−b)
Take the top-middle number ($b$). **Apply the minus sign** from the checkerboard pattern! Cross out its entire row and column.

$$
-b \times \text{Det} \begin{vmatrix} 
d & f \\ 
g & i 
\end{vmatrix}
$$

#### Step 3: The Top-Right (+c)
Take the top-right number ($c$). Cross out its entire row and column.

$$
+c \times \text{Det} \begin{vmatrix} 
d & e \\ 
g & h 
\end{vmatrix}
$$

#### Step 4: Combine them all
Add all three pieces together for the final formula:
$$|A| = a(ei - fh) - b(di - fg) + c(dh - eg)$$


# Matrix Operations (Addition & Multiplication)

### 1. Matrix Addition 
* **The Golden Rule:** You can ONLY add matrices together if they have the exact same dimensions.
* **The Method:** Simply add the numbers that are in the exact same positions.

$$
\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 \\\\ 3 & 4 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} 5 & 6 \\\\ 7 & 8 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1+5 & 2+6 \\\\ 3+7 & 4+8 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 8 \\\\ 10 & 12 \end{bmatrix}
$$

### 2. Matrix Multiplication (Row $\times$ Column)
* **The Golden Rule:** To multiply Matrix $A \times$ Matrix $B$, the number of **columns** in A MUST equal the number of **rows** in B.
* **Exam Trick:** Write dimensions side-by-side: $(2 \times 3)$ and $(3 \times 2)$. If the "inside" numbers match, you can multiply. The "outside" numbers $(2 \times 2)$ are the size of your final answer.
* **The Method (The "Dive and Sweep"):** Multiply the Rows of the first matrix by the Columns of the second matrix, adding the products.


## The Vocabulary: Conjugate & Adjugate

### The Conjugate Matrix
Relevant for **Complex Numbers** (e.g., $3 + 4i$).
* **The Definition:** Flip the sign of the imaginary part ($i$).
* **Example:** $3 + 4i \rightarrow 3 - 4i$. Real numbers like $7$ stay $7$.

### The Adjugate Matrix (The Adjoint)
The Adjugate is the Transpose of the Cofactor matrix.

#### The 2×2 Exam Shortcut:
1.  **Swap** the numbers on the main diagonal (top-left and bottom-right).
2.  **Flip** the signs (+/-) of the other two numbers.

#### The 3×3 Adjugate Process:
1.  **Matrix of Minors:** Replace each element with the determinant of its remaining $2 \times 2$ matrix.
2.  **Cofactor Matrix:** Apply the checkerboard signs:

$$
\begin{bmatrix} + & - & + \\\\ - & + & - \\\\ + & - & + \end{bmatrix}
$$

4.  **Transpose:** Swap rows and columns.



## The Inverse of a Matrix ($A^{-1}$)
* **Core Concept:** $A \cdot A^{-1} = I$ (Identity Matrix). An inverse only exists if $\text{Det} \neq 0$.
* **The Formula:** $$A^{-1} = \frac{1}{\text{Det}(A)} \times \text{Adjugate}(A)$$



## Triangular & Orthogonal Matrices

* **Triangular Matrix:** All entries below (or above) the main diagonal are zero.
    * *Shortcut:* Determinant = product of main diagonal. Eigenvalues = the diagonal numbers.
* **Orthogonal Matrix:** $A \cdot A^T = I$.
    * *Shortcut:* Transpose is the Inverse ($A^T = A^{-1}$). Determinant is always $\pm 1$.
* **Transpose Product Rule:** $(AB)^T = B^T A^T$.



## The Cayley-Hamilton Theorem
* **The Core Rule:** Every square matrix satisfies its own characteristic equation. Replace $\lambda$ with $A$, and constants with $I$.
* **Inverse Shortcut Example:**
    1.  Eq: $A^2 - 4A + 3I = 0$
    2.  Multiply by $A^{-1}$: $A - 4I + 3A^{-1} = 0$
    3.  Isolate: $3A^{-1} = 4I - A$
    4.  Result: $A^{-1} = \frac{1}{3}(4I - A)$



## Rank of a Matrix
The number of **linearly independent** (unique) rows.
* **Full Rank:** No redundant rows.
* **Rank 1:** Only one row provides unique info (others are multiples or zeros).
* **Rank 0:** The Null Matrix (all zeros).



## Systems of Linear Equations
* **No Solution:** $\text{Rank}(A) \neq \text{Rank}([A|B])$. (Lines are parallel).
* **Infinite Solutions:** $\text{Rank}(A) = \text{Rank}([A|B]) < \text{Variables}$. (Lines are identical).
* **Unique Solution:** $\text{Rank}(A) = \text{Rank}([A|B]) = \text{Variables}$. (Lines intersect at one point).



## Practice Questions (Solved)

1.  **Trace is 12, Det is 36. Two eigenvalues are 2 and 3. Third?**
    * **Answer:** 6. (Product: $2 \times 3 \times x = 36$).
2.  **If $A^2 - 5A - 2I = 0$, find $A^{-1}$.**
    * **Answer:** $\frac{1}{2}(A - 5I)$.
3.  **Rank of Row 1: $[1,2,3]$, Row 2: $[2,4,6]$, Row 3: $[0,0,0]$?**
    * **Answer:** Rank 1. (Row 2 is just $2 \times$ Row 1).
4.  **$Ax = B$ is consistent with a unique solution if:**
    * **Answer:** $\text{Rank}(A) = \text{Rank}([A|B]) = \text{number of variables}$.
5.  **Eigenvalues 1, 2, 5. Determinant?**
    * **Answer:** 10. ($1 \times 2 \times 5$).
6.  **Rank of matrix with 3 identical non-zero rows?**
    * **Answer:** Rank 1.
7.  **Rank(A) = 2, Rank([A|B]) = 3, 3 variables. Solutions?**
    * **Answer:** 0 (No solution).
