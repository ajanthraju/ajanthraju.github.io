## MODULE 1: PROPOSITIONAL LOGIC OPERATORS

### Key Concepts & Real-World Examples
* **CONJUNCTION (AND / ∧):** True only if both statements are True. (e.g., "I will buy a burger AND fries." If I only buy a burger, I lied).
* **DISJUNCTION (OR / ∨):** True if at least one statement is True. (e.g., "I will buy a burger OR fries." If I buy just a burger, I told the truth).
* **IMPLICATION (IF-THEN / →):** * *Everyday Analogy (The Promise):* "If you get 100% on the exam (P), then I will buy you a car (Q)."
  * T → T: You get 100%, I buy the car. (Promise kept = True)
  * T → F: You get 100%, I refuse to buy the car. (Promise broken = **False**) *<- THE ONLY FALSE SCENARIO*
  * F → T: You fail, but I buy you a car anyway out of pity. (I didn't break my original rule = True)
  * F → F: You fail, I don't buy a car. (Promise kept = True)

## MODULE 2: LOGICAL EQUIVALENCES

### Key Concepts & Real-World Examples
* **DE MORGAN'S LAWS:** Distributing a negation (¬) flips the internal operators.
  * ¬(P ∨ Q) ≡ ¬P ∧ ¬Q
  * *Everyday Analogy:* If it is NOT true that "I like apples OR oranges", it means "I do NOT like apples AND I do NOT like oranges".

## MODULE 3: TAUTOLOGY AND CONTRADICTION

### Key Concepts & Real-World Examples
* **TAUTOLOGY:** A proposition that is always True.
  * *Everyday Analogy:* "It will rain today, OR it will not rain today." (P ∨ ¬P). This is guaranteed to be true no matter what happens.
* **CONTRADICTION:** A proposition that is always False. 
  * *Everyday Analogy:* "I am 10 feet tall AND I am not 10 feet tall." (P ∧ ¬P). This is impossible.

---

## MODULE 4: FIRST-ORDER LOGIC (QUANTIFIERS)

### Key Concepts & Real-World Examples
* **UNIVERSAL (∀x):** "For all x". (e.g., "All humans need water").
* **EXISTENTIAL (∃x):** "There exists an x". (e.g., "There is at least one human with green eyes").
* **NEGATING QUANTIFIERS:** * *Everyday Analogy:* How do you mathematically disprove the statement "ALL swans are white" (∀x)? You don't need to prove that "NO swans are white." You only need to find "At least ONE swan that is NOT white" (∃x).

## DAILY VERIFICATION Q&A

**Q1. Which of the following is the truth value of (P → Q) when P is False and Q is True?**
* **Answer:** True 
* **Logic:** An implication is only false if True leads to False. False leading to True does not violate the implication rule.

**Q2. According to De Morgan's Law, the expression ¬(P ∨ Q) is logically equivalent to:**
* **Answer:** ¬P ∧ ¬Q 
* **Logic:** The negation distributes to both variables, and the OR (∨) flips to an AND (∧).

**Q3. The compound proposition P ∨ ¬(P ∧ Q) is a:**
* **Answer:** Tautology 
* **Logic:** By De Morgan's, this becomes P ∨ (¬P ∨ ¬Q). Grouping the P's gives (P ∨ ¬P) ∨ ¬Q. Since (P ∨ ¬P) is always True, the entire statement evaluates to True regardless of Q.

**Q4. What is the negation of the statement: "All data engineers use SQL" (∀x, P(x))?**
* **Answer:** There exists at least one data engineer who does not use SQL. 
* **Logic:** The negation of a Universal ("All") is an Existential ("At least one"), combined with the negation of the action.
