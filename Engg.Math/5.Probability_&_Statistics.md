## Core Statistics (Mean, Median, Mode, Std Dev)
* **Mean:** The mathematical average. (Add all values, divide by total).
* **Median:** The exact middle number. *Exam Trap:* You MUST sort the list from lowest to highest first.
* **Mode:** The number that appears most frequently.
* **Standard Deviation ($\sigma$):** Measures the average distance of data points from the Mean.
  * **Step 1:** Calculate the Mean.
  * **Step 2:** Subtract the Mean from every individual number, and square the result.
  * **Step 3:** Find the average of those squared numbers. (This is the *Variance*($\sigma^2$)).
  * **Step 4:** Take the Square Root of the Variance to get $\sigma$.
  * **Shortcut Rule:** If every number in a dataset is multiplied by a constant $k$, the Standard Deviation is also multiplied by $k$.



## Conditional Probability & Bayes' Theorem
* **Conditional Probability $P(A|B)$:** The probability of Event A happening, *given that* Event B has already happened and is a confirmed fact.
  * *Formula:* $$P(A|B) = \frac{P(A \cap B)}{P(B)}$$
* **Bayes' Theorem:** Used to reverse conditional probability. If you know $P(A|B)$, Bayes' lets you calculate $P(B|A)$.
  * *The Formula:* $$P(A|B) = \frac{P(B|A) \cdot P(A)}{P(B)}$$



## Discrete Distributions (Counting whole numbers)
* **Uniform Distribution:** Every single outcome has the exact same probability (e.g., Rolling a fair 6-sided die).
  * *The Formula:* $$P(X) = \frac{1}{n}$$
* **Binomial Distribution:** Used for finding exactly $k$ successes in $n$ trials, where there are only two outcomes (Success/Failure).
  * *The Formula:* $$P(X=k) = \frac{n!}{k!(n-k)!} \cdot p^k \cdot (1-p)^{n-k}$$
* **Poisson Distribution:** Used for counting the number of events that happen within a *fixed interval of time or space* (e.g., How many server requests arrive in one minute).
  * *The Formula (L-E-K Memory Hack):* $$P(X=k) = \frac{\lambda^k \cdot e^{-\lambda}}{k!}$$

## Continuous Distributions (Measuring exact decimals/time)
* **Normal Distribution (Gaussian):** The classic "Bell Curve". Data is symmetrical around the mean.
  * *The Z-Score Formula (Value minus Average, over Spread):* $$Z = \frac{x - \mu}{\sigma}$$
  * *The 68-95-99.7 Rule:*
  * **68%** of data falls within $\pm 1$ standard deviation ($1\sigma$).
  * **95%** of data falls within $\pm 2$ standard deviations ($2\sigma$).
  * **99.7%** of data falls within $\pm 3$ standard deviations ($3\sigma$).
* **Exponential Distribution:** Measures the *amount of time you have to wait* between events in a Poisson process.
  * *The Formula (Poisson without the fraction):* $$f(x) = \lambda e^{-\lambda x}$$

## Practice Questions (Solved)
1. What is the median of the following dataset: 12, 5, 22, 8, 15, 1, 9?
**Answer:** 9. *(Sort the list first: 1, 5, 8, 9, 12, 15, 22. The middle number is 9).*

2. If every single value in a dataset is multiplied by a constant k, what happens to the Standard Deviation (σ)?
**Answer:** It is multiplied by k. *(If the numbers scale by 10, the spread between them scales by 10).*

3. Given P(A) = 0.6, P(B) = 0.5, and P(A ∩ B) = 0.2. What is the conditional probability P(A|B)?
**Answer:** 0.4. *(Formula is P(A ∩ B) / P(B). So, 0.2 / 0.5 = 0.4).*

4. Which of the following is the correct mathematical formula for Bayes' Theorem?
**Answer:** $P(A|B) = \frac{P(B|A) \cdot P(A)}{P(B)}$.

5. A fair coin is tossed $3$ times. What is the probability of getting exactly $2$ heads?
**Answer:** $\frac{3}{8}$. *(Using Binomial Formula)*.

6. A server receives an average of $4$ requests per minute. Which distribution finds the probability of exactly $6$ requests in the next minute?
**Answer:** Poisson Distribution. *(Counts discrete events in a fixed time interval)*.

7. In a standard Normal Distribution, approximately what percentage of the data falls strictly within one standard deviation ($\pm 1\sigma$) of the mean?
**Answer:** $68\%$. *(This is the first tier of the Empirical Rule)*.

8. If the Poisson distribution measures *how many* events happen in a specific hour, which continuous distribution is used to measure the *amount of time you have to wait* between those events?
**Answer:** Exponential Distribution.
